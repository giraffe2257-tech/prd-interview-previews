Section 1

1. 研究背景與目的

PRD(Product Requirements Document)是跨功能團隊在日常產品開發中最頻繁依賴的核心文件之一,同時承擔需求說明、跨角色合約、規格依據、會議錨點等多重功能。然而,作為一份「需要同時被不同角色閱讀、解讀、轉化」的文件,PRD 在實務中經常出現幾種反覆發生的現象:

  • 同一份文件被不同角色讀出不同重點,事後才發現理解落差
  • 寫得太細反而稀釋重點,寫得太模糊又容易造成重工或上線後問題
  • PRD 作為跨角色協作的起點,於實作過程中被轉譯為多份派生文件(Figma、Tech Spec、工單、Test Case 等);如何維繫從起點到實作端的一致性,是值得探討的議題

本研究希望透過實務工作者的第一手經驗,理解 PRD 在真實工作流程中如何被撰寫、閱讀、轉譯,並辨識哪些溝通痛點反覆出現、哪些已有有效的因應方式、哪些可能適合以工具或 AI 介入。研究成果將構成 King's College London MSc 學位論文的核心資料,並可能延伸為後續學術發表或工具設計提案。

Section 2

2. 為什麼邀請 PM 參與

一份 PRD 在組織中通常由「產出端」與「接收端」共同建構,其中產出端指 PRD 的撰寫者,多為產品經理(PM);接收端則指 PRD 的下游讀者與執行者,包含設計師、工程師、QA、Data Analyst 等。

「文件被寫出來時,作者腦中假設了哪些讀者、為哪些段落保留了什麼、又有意或無意省略了什麼。」

由於研究者本身的職涯方向亦將往 PM 邁進,且產品開發流程在實務上多以 PM 發想需求為起點,本研究遂以 PM 視角作為核心切入點,希望從上述這個源頭出發,理解 PRD 在後續流轉中所遇到的各種現象。PM 受訪者的第一手經驗(包括撰寫策略、判斷標準、與下游角色之間的溝通方式),構成本研究最關鍵的核心資料。

4. 訪談開場的暖身

正式進入主題之前,會請受訪者簡短分享以下兩個面向,作為訪談的脈絡背景(約 5 分鐘):

Section 5

5. 訪談會涵蓋的兩個方向

以下是主題方向,並非逐題清單。訪談過程不會照順序問完;當某一個回答已順帶涵蓋下一個問題時,便會直接跳過。

5.1 PRD 的撰寫流程(佔比最重,約 25–30 分鐘)

訪談的核心,將請受訪者帶領研究者走過最近一次撰寫 PRD 的完整過程,包括從接到需求、決定撰寫策略、到交付給設計與工程的整段流程。越具體越好,例如:

  • 撰寫過程中會開啟哪些檔案?哪些步驟最容易卡關?
  • 哪些內容會明確寫進 PRD?哪些則「靠團隊默契」、「會議口頭補充」、「預期讀者會自行補上」?
  • 如何判斷一段「寫得夠了」、可以送出?心中是否有一份隱性的 checklist?
  • 為「工程師讀者」與「設計師讀者」所寫的段落,撰寫策略是否有意識地區分?

5.2 痛點、現行因應方式、工具與 AI 的角色

  • 跨角色溝通中,最常重複出現的問題是什麼?
  • 是否曾遇過「PRD 寫得模糊、導致返工、重工、衝突或延期」的經驗?這個落差後來是如何被補起來的?
  • PRD 中所載的「預期效益/指標」於上線後由誰追蹤、由誰回報?這些報告是否會回流到 PRD?這類「跨角色責任歸屬」是否曾構成痛點?
  • 團隊發展出哪些自己的解法?哪些確實有效、哪些其實只是「形式上要做」?
  • 在 PRD 相關工作中是否曾使用 AI 工具?用於哪一段流程?(若無相關使用經驗亦完全可以,可直接告知,該段將略過)
  • 若想像有一個工具專門協助「閱讀 PRD 的人」(而非撰寫者)解決上述痛點,理想中應該長什麼樣、最不應該做什麼?
Section 6

6. 收尾將詢問的三件事

  1. 今天未談到、但對本研究主題重要的事?
  2. 是否願意分享一份匿名化的 PRD 供文件分析?(收到後將再進行一次去識別化處理)
  3. 是否有適合作為下一位受訪者的同事可推薦?(PM、Designer、Engineer、QA、DA 皆可)
Section 7

7. 訪談形式上的提醒

  • 沒有標準答案。本研究關心的是真實工作經驗,負面經驗、抱怨、未完成的想法都是珍貴資料,無須包裝。
  • 訪談中可隨時拒答任何問題,或要求暫停/中止訪談。
  • 若某段內容用畫的比用講的更清楚(流程圖、組織關係、文件對應⋯),訪談現場備有紙筆與白板可隨時使用。
  • 若方便,建議於訪談時開啟一份最近撰寫過的 PRD 作為回憶錨點,不一定要分享給研究者,能讓「最近一次撰寫 PRD」這段描述更具體即可。
  • 訪談結束後 30 天內可撤回資料,無須說明理由。

  • 如何判斷「已經讀懂、可以開始拆工單與估時」?心中是否有一份隱性的 checklist?
  • PRD 通常會被翻譯成什麼產出?(user story、tech spec、工單、流程圖、ER diagram)這個翻譯是一個人完成、還是團隊共同進行?
  • PRD 中是否存在「不太懂、但通常也不會主動詢問」的部分?背後的原因是什麼?
  • 從一份 PRD 到實際開始 coding 之間,會產生幾份「中間文件」?這些文件的關係是什麼?哪一份是真正在依賴的?
  • 當不同文件之間出現矛盾時,會以哪一份為準?背後的判斷邏輯是什麼?
  • 在後端/分散式系統的脈絡下,「系統現在實際在跑什麼」的真正答案,在於 code base 本身,還是 code + runtime config(env var / feature flag)+ 當下部署的 version 三者合一?

  • 跨角色溝通中,最常重複出現的問題是什麼?哪些誤解「文件再怎麼寫也救不了」?
  • 是否曾遇過「PRD 寫得模糊、導致返工、重工、衝突或延期」的經驗?這個落差後來是如何被補起來的?

  • PRD、Figma、Test Case 最終需被整合成「工程師真正在依賴的東西」時,這項整合工作應該由誰來做?PM?AI?工程師自己?

5.3 開發後流程與品質把關(Backend 的重點段落)

  • 部署 / 上線:feature 通過 QA 後如何上線?全量?灰度?feature flag?每日平均部署幾次?rollback 在哪些條件下會被觸發?
  • 上線後監測:如何確認 feature 真的依照預期運作?PRD 中所載指標由誰追蹤、由誰回報?這些報告是否會回流到 PRD?
  • Incident response:是否有上線後出 incident 的經驗?事後的 postmortem 是否會回頭影響 PRD 的寫法?
  • 跨團隊 API(本研究對 Backend 的重點段落):
    • 與其他 team 共用的 API 規範寫在哪裡?與 PRD 是同一份還是另一份?
    • 對方 team 變更 API 而未通知、造成自家系統受影響,這類事件多久發生一次?團隊如何避免?

Feature 04 · For Backend Engineer Participants · English

Backend Engineer (English)

The backend engineer's perspective shows how design intent becomes the actual system behaviour layered into code, runtime configuration, and the deployed version.

Duration
60–75 min
Language
Primarily Mandarin (switch to English as useful)
Format
Semi-structured
Location
Online or in person
Section 1

1. Research Background and Aim

The Product Requirements Document (PRD) is one of the most frequently relied-upon documents in day-to-day cross-functional product development. As a document that is "read, interpreted, and transformed by different roles at the same time", PRDs in practice repeatedly produce several recognisable patterns:

  • The same document is read differently by different roles, with discrepancies in understanding only surfacing later
  • Writing in too much detail dilutes the key points, while writing too ambiguously causes rework or post-launch issues
  • As the starting point of cross-role collaboration, a PRD is translated during implementation into multiple derivative documents (Figma, Tech Spec, tickets, Test Cases, etc.)
Section 2

2. Why Backend Engineers Are Invited

A PRD is typically co-constructed by a "producing side" (the writer, usually the PM) and a "receiving side" (downstream readers and executors, including designers, engineers, QA, and Data Analysts).

"How design intent becomes the actual system behaviour layered into code, runtime configuration, and the deployed version."

The backend engineer's perspective offers a view of this transformation. In addition, backend work frequently involves sharing APIs across teams and handling schema changes — situations where the PM often lacks visibility, and where scenarios described in the PRD cannot actually be carried out.

Section 3

3. Practical Information

ItemDetails
Duration60–75 minutes (flexible)
FormatSemi-structured interview
LocationOnline (Zoom / Meet / Teams) or in person
RecordingWith verbal consent, audio-recorded for transcription and analysis only
LanguagePrimarily Mandarin, switching to English where useful
Section 4

4. Warm-Up at the Start of the Interview

Before moving into the main topics, the participant will be invited to briefly share (about 5 minutes):

  1. Participant background: current company or industry, current position, and time in this position
  2. Team's product development flow: roughly what the team's day-to-day workflow looks like
Section 5

5. The Three Topic Areas

The items below are topic directions, not a list of questions to be asked one by one. For backend participants, sections 5.1 and 5.3 will take the most time.

5.1 Reading the PRD and the downstream artefacts produced from it (~20–25 min)

  • What is the first action taken upon receiving a PRD? Read top to bottom? Jump straight to the acceptance criteria? Find someone to ask first?
  • Which step tends to get most stuck?
  • What kinds of content are not written in the PRD but have to be decided independently?
  • Which sections are deliberately skipped? Has skipping them ever caused regret?
  • How is the threshold of "understood enough to break down tickets and estimate work" judged?
  • What artefacts does a PRD typically get translated into? Is the translation done individually, or as a team?
  • Are there parts of the PRD that are "not really understood, but usually not asked about either"?
  • Between receiving a PRD and starting to write code, how many "intermediate documents" are typically produced? Which one is actually relied on?
  • When different documents contradict each other, which one is treated as authoritative?
  • In a backend / distributed systems context, where is the real answer to "what is the system actually doing right now"? In the code base, or in code + runtime config + the version currently deployed taken together?

5.2 Pain points, current coping strategies, the role of tools and AI

  • What is the issue that recurs most often in cross-role communication?
  • Has there ever been an experience where "an ambiguous PRD led to rework, conflict, or delay"? How was that gap filled?
  • The most difficult edge case ever encountered: did it come from genuinely complex business logic, or from spilling into another team's scope?
  • What workarounds has the team developed? Which actually work, and which are only "done for form's sake"?
  • Have any AI tools been used in PRD-related work? (No experience is fine — the topic can simply be skipped.)
  • Imagine a tool dedicated to helping the readers of PRDs (rather than the writers): what should it ideally look like, and what should it definitely not do?
  • When PRDs, Figma, and Test Cases need to be integrated into "what engineers actually rely on", who should do that integration work?

5.3 Post-implementation flow and quality control (the key section for backend)

  • Code review / PR flow: What happens between finishing coding and merging to main? Does code review surface inconsistencies with the PRD, or only focus on technical quality?
  • Deployment / release: Once a feature passes QA, how does it go live? Full rollout? Gradual? Feature flag? Under what conditions is a rollback triggered?
  • Post-launch monitoring: How is it confirmed that the feature is actually behaving as expected? Who tracks the metrics specified in the PRD?
  • Incident response: Do post-mortems ever feed back into how PRDs are written?
  • Cross-team APIs:
    • Where are the API specifications shared with other teams documented? Same PRD or separate?
    • How often does another team change an API without notice? How does the team try to prevent this?
    • What is the coordination process for "cross-team changes" such as schema migrations and dependency upgrades?
Section 6

6. The Three Things Asked at the End

  1. Anything not covered today that feels important for this research topic?
  2. Would there be willingness to share an anonymised PRD (or accompanying tech spec / API doc) for document analysis?
  3. Are there colleagues who might be suitable as the next interviewees?
Section 7

7. A Few Notes on Interview Format

  • There is no right answer. Negative experiences, complaints, and unfinished thoughts are all valuable material; no polishing is needed.
  • Any question can be declined at any time; the interview can be paused or stopped at any point.
  • If something is clearer to draw than to describe, pen and paper or a whiteboard will be available on the spot.
  • If convenient, having a recently worked-on PRD and its accompanying tech spec / tickets open during the interview is useful as a memory anchor.
  • Data can be withdrawn within 30 days of the interview, without giving a reason.
Section 8 · Optional

8. Phase Two (Optional)

After common patterns are identified from the first phase, a proposed improvement will be developed, and participants will be invited back for one feedback interview (about 45–60 minutes). Whether to participate in the second phase can be decided after the first-phase interview is complete.

For any questions about this document, or anything that would be useful to clarify in advance, please feel free to reply. Many thanks for taking the time.

Section 1

1. 研究背景與目的

PRD(Product Requirements Document)是跨功能團隊在日常產品開發中最頻繁依賴的核心文件之一。然而,作為一份「需要同時被不同角色閱讀、解讀、轉化」的文件,PRD 在實務中經常出現幾種反覆發生的現象:

  • 同一份文件被不同角色讀出不同重點,事後才發現理解落差
  • 寫得太細反而稀釋重點,寫得太模糊又容易造成重工或上線後問題
  • PRD 作為跨角色協作的起點,於實作過程中被轉譯為多份派生文件
Section 2

2. 為什麼邀請此身份的受訪者

對本研究而言,同時兼任工程師與 Tech PM 兩種身分的受訪者是極為珍貴的觀察對象,原因有二:

  1. 同一個人具備跨角色的內在視角。純 PM 或純 RD 的訪談只能各自呈現單面視角,而兼具兩種身分的受訪者,能直接描述從接收端切換到產出端時思考方式的差異。
  2. iOS(mobile)平台的特殊性。派生文件鏈(常以 Figma 為核心)、上線流程(App Store 審查、舊版相容)、SSOT 結構(code + App Store 上線版本 + remote config / feature flag)都有其獨特之處。

考量到受訪者的工作時間配比約為 iOS 工程師 70%、Tech PM 30%,訪談內容也會大致對應這個比例:iOS RD 視角佔最大比重,Tech PM 視角為第二主軸,最後再進入痛點與 iOS 開發後流程。
