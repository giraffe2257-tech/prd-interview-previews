  1. 受訪者背景:目前服務的公司或產業類型、現在的職位,以及在這個職位上的年資

2. 訪談方向

以下是主題方向,並非逐題清單。訪談過程不會照順序問完;當某一個回答已順帶涵蓋下一個問題時,便會直接跳過。

PRD 的撰寫流程(佔比最重,約 25–30 分鐘)

請受訪者帶領研究者走過最近一次撰寫 PRD 的完整過程,包括從接到需求、決定撰寫策略、到交付給設計與工程的整段流程。越具體越好,例如:

  • 撰寫過程中會開啟哪些檔案?哪些步驟最容易卡關?
  • 哪些內容會明確寫進 PRD?哪些則「靠團隊默契」、「會議口頭補充」、「預期讀者會自行補上」?
  • 如何判斷一段「寫得夠了」、可以送出?心中是否有一份隱性的 checklist?
  • 為「工程師讀者」與「設計師讀者」所寫的段落,撰寫策略是否有意識地區分?

痛點、工具與 AI 的角色

  • 跨角色溝通中,最常重複出現的問題是什麼?
  • 是否曾遇過「PRD 寫得模糊、導致返工、重工、衝突或延期」的經驗?這個落差後來是如何被補起來的?
  • PRD 中所載的「預期效益/指標」於上線後由誰追蹤、由誰回報?這些報告是否會回流到 PRD?這類「跨角色責任歸屬」是否曾構成痛點?
  • 團隊發展出哪些自己的解法?哪些確實有效、哪些其實只是「形式上要做」?
  • 在 PRD 相關工作中是否曾使用 AI 工具?用於哪一段流程?(若無相關使用經驗亦完全可以,可直接告知,該段將略過)
  • 若想像有一個工具專門協助「閱讀 PRD 的人」(而非撰寫者)解決上述痛點,理想中應該長什麼樣、最不應該做什麼?
Section 3

  1. 今天未談到、但對本研究主題重要的事?
  2. 是否願意分享一份匿名化的 PRD 供文件分析?(收到後將再進行一次去識別化處理)
  3. 是否有適合作為下一位受訪者的同事可推薦?(PM、Designer、Engineer、QA、DA 皆可)
Section 4

4. 形式提醒

  • 沒有標準答案。本研究關心的是真實工作經驗,負面經驗、抱怨、未完成的想法都是珍貴資料,無須包裝。
  • 訪談中可隨時拒答任何問題,或要求暫停/中止訪談。
  • 若某段內容用畫的比用講的更清楚(流程圖、組織關係、文件對應⋯),訪談現場備有紙筆與白板可隨時使用。
  • 若方便,建議於訪談時開啟一份最近撰寫過的 PRD 作為回憶錨點,不一定要分享給研究者,能讓「最近一次撰寫 PRD」這段描述更具體即可。
  • 訪談結束後 30 天內可撤回資料,無須說明理由。

Feature 01 · For PM Participants

Product Manager

The study examines the readers a PRD author silently assumes, what is preserved in each section, and what is intentionally or unintentionally omitted. The PM perspective is the core entry point of this research.

Duration
60–75 min
Language
Primarily Mandarin (switch to English as useful)
Format
Semi-structured
Location
Online or in person
Interview Guide · Section 1

1. Warm-Up

Before moving into the main topics, the participant will be invited to briefly share (about 5 minutes):

  1. Participant background: current company or industry, current position, and time in this position
  2. Team's product development flow: roughly what the team's day-to-day workflow looks like
Section 2

2. Topic Areas

The items below are topic directions, not a list of questions to be asked one by one. When an answer already covers another question, it will simply be skipped.

The PRD writing flow (the largest block, ~25–30 min)

The participant will be invited to walk the researcher through the most recent PRD they wrote, end to end — from receiving the requirement, deciding on a writing strategy, to handing off to design and engineering. The more specific the better:

  • What files are opened during the writing process? Which steps tend to get most stuck?
  • What content is explicitly written into the PRD? What is left to "team tacit knowledge", "verbal additions in meetings", or "expected readers will fill in themselves"?
  • How is the threshold of "enough has been written" judged? Is there an implicit checklist?
  • For sections aimed at "engineer readers" versus "designer readers", is the writing strategy consciously differentiated?

Pain points, tools, and the role of AI

  • What is the issue that recurs most often in cross-role communication?
  • Has there ever been an experience where "an ambiguous PRD led to rework, conflict, or delay"? How was that gap later filled?
  • For the "expected outcomes / metrics" stated in a PRD, who tracks them post-launch, and who reports back? Do these reports feed back into the PRD? Has this cross-role accountability ever been a pain point?
  • What workarounds has the team developed? Which actually work, and which are only "done for form's sake"?
  • Have any AI tools been used in PRD-related work? Which part of the flow? (No experience is fine — the topic can simply be skipped.)
  • Imagine a tool dedicated to helping the readers of PRDs (rather than the writers): what should it ideally look like, and what should it definitely not do?
Section 3

3. Closing Questions

  1. Anything not covered today that feels important for this research topic?
  2. Would there be willingness to share an anonymised PRD for document analysis? (A second de-identification pass will be applied upon receipt.)
  3. Are there colleagues who might be suitable as the next interviewees? (PM, Designer, Engineer, QA, DA are all welcome.)
Section 4

4. Format Notes

  • There is no right answer. The study cares about real working experience — negative experiences, complaints, and unfinished thoughts are all valuable material; no polishing is needed.
  • Any question can be declined at any time; the interview can be paused or stopped at any point.
  • If something is clearer to draw than to describe (flowcharts, organisational relationships, document mappings…), pen and paper or a whiteboard will be available on the spot.
  • If convenient, having a recently written PRD open during the interview is useful as a memory anchor; it does not need to be shared, but it helps make "the most recent PRD I wrote" more concrete.
  • Data can be withdrawn within 30 days of the interview, without giving a reason.
Section 5 · Optional

5. Phase Two

After common patterns are identified from the first phase, a proposed improvement will be developed, and participants will be invited back for one feedback interview (about 45–60 minutes). Whether to participate in the second phase can be decided after the first-phase interview is complete.

For any questions about this document, or anything that would be useful to clarify in advance, please feel free to reply. Many thanks for taking the time.

Feature 02 · For Designer Participants

Designer

Designers are at once readers of PRDs and producers of derivative documents such as Figma and design specs — sitting in a uniquely transitional position within the cross-role translation chain.

Duration
60–75 min
Language
Primarily Mandarin (switch to English as useful)
Format
Semi-structured
Location
Online or in person
Interview Guide · Section 1

1. Warm-Up

Before moving into the main topics, the participant will be invited to briefly share (about 5 minutes):

  1. Participant background: current company or industry, current position, and time in this position
  2. Team's product development flow: roughly what the team's day-to-day workflow looks like
Section 2

2. Topic Areas

The items below are topic directions, not a list of questions to be asked one by one. When an answer already covers another question, it will simply be skipped.

Reading PRDs and producing design specs (the largest block, ~25–30 min)

The participant will be invited to walk the researcher through the most recent end-to-end workflow after receiving a PRD, from opening the PRD to producing design specs / wireframes / Figma. The more specific the better:

  • What is the first action taken upon receiving a PRD? Read top to bottom? Jump to specific sections? Find someone to ask first?
  • Which step tends to get most stuck — unclear thinking, missing information, or PRD silence?
  • What content isn't written in the PRD but has to be filled in independently? (Behavioural motivation, context, brand tone, interaction details…)
  • Which sections are deliberately skipped? Has skipping them ever caused regret?
  • When reading a PRD, what other documents or tools are opened alongside? (Past PRDs, design system, user research, Slack threads…)
  • How is "understood enough to start drawing" judged? Is there an implicit checklist?
  • Are PRDs received text-heavy or richly illustrated? Which is easier to work with? Why?
  • Are there parts of the PRD that are "not really understood, but usually not asked about either"? Why?

Pain points, tools, and the role of AI

  • What is the issue that recurs most often in cross-role communication? Which misunderstandings "no amount of writing can fix"?
  • Has there ever been an experience where "an ambiguous PRD led to rework, conflict, or delay"? How was that gap later filled?
  • Conversely, are there ambiguous sections in a PRD that designers enjoy interpreting freely? Why?
  • What workarounds has the team developed? Which actually work, and which are only "done for form's sake"?
  • Have any AI tools been used in PRD-related work? (No experience is fine.)
  • Imagine a tool dedicated to helping the readers of PRDs (rather than the writers): what should it ideally look like, and what should it definitely not do?
  • When PRDs, Figma, and Test Cases need to be integrated into "what's actually relied on", who should do that integration work? PM? AI? Engineers themselves?
Section 3

3. Closing Questions

  1. Anything not covered today that feels important for this research topic?
  2. Would there be willingness to share an anonymised PRD (or its accompanying Figma spec) for document analysis? (A second de-identification pass will be applied upon receipt.)
  3. Are there colleagues who might be suitable as the next interviewees? (PM, Designer, Engineer, QA, DA are all welcome.)
Section 4

4. Format Notes

  • There is no right answer. The study cares about real working experience — negative experiences, complaints, and unfinished thoughts are all valuable material; no polishing is needed.
  • Any question can be declined at any time; the interview can be paused or stopped at any point.
  • If something is clearer to draw than to describe (flowcharts, document mappings, Figma structure…), pen and paper or a whiteboard will be available.
  • If convenient, having a recently worked-on PRD and its accompanying Figma open during the interview is useful as a memory anchor.
  • Data can be withdrawn within 30 days of the interview, without giving a reason.
Section 5 · Optional

5. Phase Two

After common patterns are identified from the first phase, a proposed improvement will be developed, and participants will be invited back for one feedback interview (about 45–60 minutes). Whether to participate in the second phase can be decided after the first-phase interview is complete.

Backend Engineer — 後端工程師

後端工程師的視角能讓研究者看見:當一份 PRD 進入實作端後,設計意圖如何轉化為 code、runtime config 與部署版本三者疊加的真實系統行為。

Duration
60–75 min
Language
中文(可切換英文)
Format
Semi-structured
Location
Online or in person
訪綱 · Section 1

1. 開場暖身

正式進入主題之前,會請受訪者簡短分享以下兩個面向(約 5 分鐘):

  1. 受訪者背景:目前服務的公司或產業類型、現在的職位,以及在這個職位上的年資
  2. 團隊的產品開發流程:目前團隊做產品時大致的工作流程長什麼樣子
Section 2

2. 訪談方向

以下是主題方向,並非逐題清單。對 Backend 而言,第 1 與第 3 部分將會花費較多時間。

PRD 的閱讀流程與後續產出(約 20–25 分鐘)

請受訪者帶領研究者走過最近一次拿到 PRD 之後的完整工作流程,包括從打開 PRD、到拆出工單/寫完 tech spec、開始實作的整段過程。越具體越好,例如:

  • 拿到 PRD 的第一個動作是什麼?從頭讀?直接跳到 acceptance criteria?先找人問?
  • 哪一個步驟最容易卡關?
  • PRD 上沒寫、卻必須自行決定的內容通常是什麼?(資料結構、錯誤碼、API 介面、非功能需求⋯)
  • 哪些段落會主動跳過?跳過後是否曾後悔?
  • 如何判斷「已經讀懂、可以開始拆工單與估時」?心中是否有一份隱性的 checklist?
  • PRD 通常會被翻譯成什麼產出?(user story、tech spec、工單、流程圖、ER diagram)這個翻譯是一個人完成、還是團隊共同進行?
  • PRD 中是否存在「不太懂、但通常也不會主動詢問」的部分?背後的原因是什麼?
  • 從一份 PRD 到實際開始 coding 之間,會產生幾份「中間文件」?這些文件的關係是什麼?哪一份是真正在依賴的?
  • 當不同文件之間出現矛盾時,會以哪一份為準?背後的判斷邏輯是什麼?
  • 在後端/分散式系統的脈絡下,「系統現在實際在跑什麼」的真正答案,在於 code base 本身,還是 code + runtime config(env var / feature flag)+ 當下部署的 version 三者合一?

痛點、工具與 AI 的角色

  • 跨角色溝通中,最常重複出現的問題是什麼?哪些誤解「文件再怎麼寫也救不了」?
  • 是否曾遇過「PRD 寫得模糊、導致返工、重工、衝突或延期」的經驗?這個落差後來是如何被補起來的?
  • 印象中最棘手的 edge case:根源是「業務邏輯本身複雜」,還是「分岔到別人團隊的 scope」?這類痛點如何被治理?
  • 團隊發展出哪些自己的解法?哪些確實有效、哪些其實只是「形式上要做」?
  • 在 PRD 相關工作中是否曾使用 AI 工具?用於哪一段流程?(若無相關使用經驗亦完全可以)
  • 若想像有一個工具專門協助「閱讀 PRD 的人」(而非撰寫者)解決上述痛點,理想中應該長什麼樣、最不應該做什麼?
  • PRD、Figma、Test Case 最終需被整合成「工程師真正在依賴的東西」時,這項整合工作應該由誰來做?PM?AI?工程師自己?

開發後流程與品質把關(Backend 的重點段落)

  • Code review / PR 流程:完成 coding 到合入 main branch 之間發生了什麼?code review 是否會挑出「與 PRD 不一致」之處,或僅關注技術品質?

  • Code review / PR flow: What happens between finishing coding and merging to main? Does code review surface inconsistencies with the PRD, or only focus on technical quality?
  • Deployment / release: Once a feature passes QA, how does it go live? Full rollout? Gradual? Feature flag? Under what conditions is a rollback triggered?

  • Incident response: Do post-mortems ever feed back into how PRDs are written?
  • Cross-team APIs:
    • Where are the API specifications shared with other teams documented? Same PRD or separate?
